3D printing is the process by which three-dimensional objects are formed from a digital model using a variety of UV-cured layered resins.
Resins offered include:
  • Acrylonitrile butadiene styrene resin (ABS) - This lightweight thermoplastic polymer is useful for a wide variety of applications due to its high-strength, impact resistance and insulating properties.
  • Flexible resin - This resin has excellent tear strength and mimics the properties of rubber to create firm but flexible rubber-like parts.
  • Plant-based resin - As the name suggests, this is a natural bio-based renewable plastic resin.
  • Standard resin - Is a fast-curing photopolymer resin, it is the most commonly used resin due to its low-cost and versatility.

Direct-to-Film is a form of transfer printing that allows for prints to be transferred onto a variety of different surfaces using polyethylene terephthalate film and water based inks.
DTF printing is a semi-permanent option that typically lasts for up to 60-70 wash cycles.

Sublimation printing is a form of printing that uses a transfer film and heat to embed sublimation ink directly into the material being printed on. As a result sublimation prints lasts longer than HTV and DTF prints. Sublimation is most effective when used on polyester coated surfaces or materials with at least 70% polyester.
